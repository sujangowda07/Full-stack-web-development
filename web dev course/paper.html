Infosys' BIG Decision on Work From Office
Mandate Amid Narayana Murthy's 70-Hour Work Week Call

Infosys, India’s second-largest technology outsourcing company, is urging its employees to return to the office at least three days a week, with plans to make this mandatory in the near future.
The call for in-person attendance follows management's unsuccessful attempts to restore a regular office routine.

Infosys cofounder NR Narayana Murthy has emphasized the importance of a 70-hour work week to boost productivity and economic growth, even as employees resist a return to the traditional office environment.

ET in its report quoted an email from vertical heads of the Bengaluru-based software company which stated, “Please start coming to the office at least 3 days a week. It will become mandatory very shortly.” Infosys has not responded to requests for comment.

Singapore On High Alert
As Covid Cases Hit Record High For 2023

Singapore hospitals are closely monitoring the recent surge in COVID-19 cases, ready
to expand capacity if necessary. Over the past two weeks, the country has experienced
an increase in infections, with 32,035 cases reported from November 26 to December 2 compared to 22,094 in the previous week, according to
the Ministry of Health.

While hospitalisation and ICU cases are not as high as during the pandemic's peak, the rising cases have intensified the workload in already busy hospitals. The National University Health System (NUHS) and SingHealth are actively monitoring the situation.
